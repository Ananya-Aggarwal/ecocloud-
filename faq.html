Q1: What is climate change?
Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric conditions on Earth. It is largely driven by human activities, such as the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, which result in the increased concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.
Q2: What are greenhouse gases, and how do they contribute to climate change?
Greenhouse gases are gases that trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to the warming of the planet. The main greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), and fluorinated gases. Human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, release large amounts of these gases, enhancing the natural greenhouse effect and causing global temperatures to rise.
Q3: How does climate change impact the environment?
Climate change has widespread and varied impacts on the environment. It leads to rising sea levels, more frequent and severe weather events, disruptions in ecosystems, loss of biodiversity, and changes in precipitation patterns. These impacts can affect agriculture, water resources, human health, and the overall balance of ecosystems.
